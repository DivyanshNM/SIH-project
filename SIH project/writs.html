Writs

A writ is a formal written order issued by a court or other legal authority. It commands the recipient to perform or refrain from performing a specific act. Writs are used to enforce or protect legal rights and are often associated with various types of legal proceedings. Writs play a crucial role in ensuring legal compliance and protecting individual rights within the judicial system.
