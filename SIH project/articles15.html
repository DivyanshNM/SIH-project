Article 15:Prohibition of Discrimination

The State shall not discriminate against any citizen on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth or any of them.

Article 15 makes it illegal for the government to treat people differently just because of their religion, race, caste, gender, or where they were born. This is important because it helps to ensure that everyone has the same access to public places, schools, and services. However, the government can make special provisions for certain groups like women.
