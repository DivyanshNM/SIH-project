Fundamental rights

Are the basic human rights guaranteed by the Constitution to all citizens, ensuring their freedom, dignity, and equality. These rights include freedom of speech, right to equality, right to privacy, and protection against discrimination, among others. They serve as a safeguard against any unjust actions by the state and help in promoting a just and fair society. In essence, fundamental rights are the backbone of a democratic nation, ensuring that every individual can live with dignity and freedom.
